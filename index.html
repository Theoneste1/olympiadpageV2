About RwMO

The Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad (RwMO) fosters mathematical excellence by identifying and nurturing talent among students across the country. It enhances problem-solving skills, inspires STEM careers, and improves mathematics education. RwMO offers opportunities for students to compete nationally and internationally, building a sense of community and promoting collaboration. By nurturing future leaders in STEM, RwMO contributes to Rwanda's development. Committed to inclusivity, RwMO ensures all students have the opportunity to participate, promoting equal opportunity and discovering hidden talents. Join RwMO to empower students, strengthen education, and contribute to Rwanda's growth in STEM fields.

Our Vision

Fostering a culture of mathematical excellence and innovation among Rwandan students with the aims of empowering them to become leaders in STEM fields and make meaningful contributions to societal advancement.

Our Mission

We want to identify and nurture mathematical talent among Rwandan students through engaging competitions and training programs. We promote problem-solving skills, critical thinking, and creativity, inspiring a passion for mathematics and STEM education.

Our Values

we uphold values of excellence, equity, innovation, and collaboration. Our commitment to integrity, diversity, and continuous improvement guides our actions and decisions.

OUR ACTIVITIES

RwMO Activities

Rwanda Mathematics Competition(RMC)

Feb-May

RwMO facilitates the Rwanda Mathematics Competition (RMC) for Juniors (S1-S3) and Seniors (S4-S5) across the country. The competition included three rounds with three-hour exams for all Rwandan high schools. Top students from each round advance, with 10,000 juniors and 4,000 seniors moving to Round 2, and 600 juniors and 400 seniors reaching Round 3, with equal representation of boys and girls at each stage. After Round 3, over 90 students, including 50 juniors and 40 seniors, are invited to a physical camp for continuous training to enhance their Olympiad mathematics skills. Successful participants form Team Rwanda to attend international competitions like PAMO, IMO, and EAMO. Camps and Ongoing Training

Jul-Dec

The Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad hosts both online and physical camps, typically lasting one week at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS). These camps gather around 45 students to engage in open discussions and debates on various mathematical concepts and theorems. They aim to deepen understanding, enhance problem-solving skills, and make mathematics more accessible and exciting. For students unable to attend, resources are available on the Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad website to continue enhancing their problem-solving abilities and prepare for future competitions. Additionally, every year, we support six students to participate in the International Summer Camp, further enriching their mathematical knowledge and skills on a global stage.

International Mathematics Competitions

April-August

The Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad (RwMO) plays a pivotal role in preparing Team Rwanda for participation in prestigious international Mathematics Competitions such as PAMO, IMO, and EAMO. We meticulously select talented participants, provide comprehensive training sessions to hone their mathematical skills, offer logistical support for their travel arrangements, and ensure they are thoroughly prepared to compete at these global events, showcasing Rwanda's talent and commitment to excellence in mathematics.

Coach Trainings

All Year Long

The Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad (RwMO) offers coach training programs designed to prepare individuals for roles as Olympiad coaches. These comprehensive programs include instruction in advanced mathematical concepts, -solving techniques, effective coaching methods, resource management, motivational strategies, and professional skills. They also foster collaboration and networking opportunities among coaches. This training guarantees high-quality mentorship, thereby improving students' competition outcomes and overall mathematical growth. Registration is open to anyone interested in becoming an Olympiad coach.

Rwanda Mathematics Competition (RMC)

The Rwanda Mathematics Competition (RMC) is a nationwide event that involves over 30,000 junior students (S1-S3) and over 8,000 senior students (S4-S5) from more than 900 high schools across the country. The competition is structured into three rounds, each consisting of a three-hour exam.

Competition Structure

Round 1:

  • Participation: 30,000 juniors and 8,000 seniors.
  • Format: Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs) marked centrally using a bot.
  • Location: Held in schools.
  • Advancement: The top 10,000 juniors and 4,000 seniors move to Round 2.

Round 2:

  • Format: MCQs marked centrally using a bot.
  • Location: Held in schools.
  • Advancement: The top 600 juniors and 400 seniors move to Round 3.

Round 3:

  • Format: More advanced exams.
  • Location: Held in schools.
  • Advancement: The top 50 juniors and 40 seniors are invited to a physical camp.

Final Training Camp:

  • Participants: Over 90 students, including 50 juniors and 40 seniors.
  • Objective: To provide continuous training to enhance their Olympiad mathematics skills.
  • Equal Representation: The competition ensures equal representation of boys and girls at each stage.

Notes: The RMC reaches approximately 80% of secondary schools in Rwanda and we are still trying to increase this number of participants to +90% by 2026 . The competition is held between February and May each year, in collaboration with the University of Rwanda and the University of Waterloo, supporting the competition.

Online Mentorship:

RwMO in-person and online camps:

The Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad hosts online mentorship to the students who are going for the international Mathematics Competitions.

Our students engage in self-directed learning to maximize their potential in mathematical problem-solving, focusing particularly on preparing for competitions such as the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO). They utilize a blend of technological tools and traditional methods to enhance their learning experiences.

Heres how we conduct our online training:

Enhancing Student Engagement with Remote Learning:

  • Motivation Strategies: We design activities and materials to keep students motivated to learn remotely, leveraging upcoming competitions and events to inspire self-motivation.
  • Cultural and Socioeconomic Considerations: We analyze and address cultural and socioeconomic factors influencing student engagement, tailoring our approaches accordingly.

Facilitating Remote Coach-Student Interactions:

  • Communication Tools: We utilize various tools such as video calls, chat platforms, and forums to maintain effective communication between students and coaches.
  • Collaborative Platforms: We use online collaboration tools to facilitate meaningful interactions, ensuring continuous support and guidance regardless of geographical distance.
  • Platform Selection: We identify and evaluate online platforms suitable for students based on language levels, educational backgrounds, math proficiency, and access to technology.
  • Usability and Accessibility: We assess the usability and accessibility of platforms to ensure they meet students' needs and enhance their learning experience.
  • Resource Provision: We provide access to effective and user-friendly online learning platforms, forums, and educational resources.

Notes:By focusing on these areas, we create a robust and supportive online training environment that maximizes our students' potential and prepares them for success in mathematical competitions.

Our story in International Mathematics competitions

Team Rwanda Mathematics Olympiad actively participates in several prestigious international Math Olympiads , including the East African Math Olympiad (EAMO), Pan-African Math Olympiad (PAMO), and International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO). Additionally, we currently attend the East Africa Math Olympiad online and aspire to join the European Girls' Mathematical Olympiad in the future. Each year, approximately 20 talented students are selected to represent Rwanda in these esteemed competitions, providing them with invaluable opportunities to showcase their mathematical abilities and passion.

Rwanda has swiftly risen to prominence among Africa's top-performing nations in these competitions, demonstrating remarkable progress year after year. Our achievements speak volumes:

  • Achieving an impressive 4th place in PAMO 2023, following only Morocco, South Africa, and Algeria
  • Securing 4 Silver, 5 Bronze medals, and earning 2 Honourable Mentions in PAMO over the past three years
  • Garnering 4 Honourable Mentions in the IMO during the same period
  • Excelling better than 24.3% of teams globally at the IMO
  • Tying for 1st place in EAMO, even with our development team's participation

Highlighting our commitment to excellence, Rwanda proudly hosted PAMO 2023 in Kigali with just six months' preparation. This event not only showcased the exceptional talent of Rwandan and African participants but also marked a significant milestone:

  • Expanding participation from 9 countries in 2022 to an impressive 31 countries and welcoming 180 students
  • Introducing innovative additions to PAMO, including Virtual Participation, a Careers Fair, Excursions, Games Rooms, and Math on the Street
  • Live-streaming the event to a global audience, shining a spotlight on African achievements in mathematics

Currently, our team leaders and deputies are selected from our pool of coaches, encouraging them to improve and providing leadership opportunities. We strategically choose younger or developing students for EAMO and PAMO to enhance their likelihood of performing strongly at the IMO in subsequent years.

These accomplishments underscore Rwanda's dedication to nurturing mathematical talent and promoting excellence on the international stage. We look forward to continuing our journey of success and inspiring future generations of mathematicians across Africa and beyond.
